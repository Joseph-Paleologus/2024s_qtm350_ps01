DraftKings DKNG 1.08%increase; green up pointing triangle plans to acquire lottery app Jackpocket for about $750 million in cash and stock in a deal that will allow the sports-betting giant to expand into lottery games.

The company said it would pay about 55% of the consideration in cash from its balance sheet. The remainder will be payable in common stock.

DraftKings said the deal would allow it to “access and grow into the massive U.S. lottery industry.” The deal will also strengthen its position in sports betting and online gaming by increasing customer lifetime value and enhancing customer acquisition.

DraftKings said it expected the deal to drive $260 million to $340 million in incremental revenue in fiscal 2026, and $350 million to $450 million in revenue in fiscal 2028.
